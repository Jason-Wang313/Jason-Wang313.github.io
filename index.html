About

Adaptive intelligence for the physical world.

I am a Computer Science student at the University of Cambridge interested in adaptive physical world models, embodied AI, compositional generalization, novelty detection, active exploration, continual learning, and sim-to-real robot adaptation.

My research interests have fully shifted toward physical world models and embodied intelligence, after earlier work in AI safety, alignment, benchmark design, and theoretical ML. I am ranked 1/128 in Cambridge Computer Science.

Research Agenda

Composing mechanisms, detecting novelty, and learning from evidence.

My long-term research goal is to build adaptive physical world models that can compose known mechanisms, identify missing physical concepts, actively gather evidence, and continually update for robust open-world robot generalization.
